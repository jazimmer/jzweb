About Elly Reviews and Characters

Kenneth Turan, writing in the LA Times, has an interesting review.

And here is another review from Roger Ebert.

"About Elly" is a bit hard to follow because so many characters play a significant role. The movie throws you into the middle of a bunch of friends just as Elly is. Only it is harder for us because the Iranian names are so unfamiliar. If there is a principle protagonist, it isn't Elly but rather Sepideh who has invited Elly on a trip to the Caspian sea with the intention of bringing Elly and Sepideh's friend Ahmad together. (Elly only appears a few times in the movie.)

Ahmad is visiting from Germany and is recently divorced.

Sepideh is married to Amir who is a bit fed up with his wife's machinations.

All in all there are three married couples. Peyman and Shohreh are perhaps the second most important couple in the movie. One of their children will be in danger of drowning.

Five characters are enough to think about before the movie starts. Try to remember their names: Amir and Sepideh, Peyman and Shohreh, and the divorced Ahmad. Of course, there is also Elly whom you won't see much of.

You get a real taste of social lying and group decision making, Iranian style, in this movie.
