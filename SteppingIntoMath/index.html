Introduction

How is that mathematicians can create precise, universally accepted Truths?

For me the answer starts with the fact that mathematicians need to be able to convince all credible sceptics that their work is correct. Mathematical convincing has been done across thousands of years and between very different cultures. No surprise then that the technique of mathematical reasoning has evolved into something very, very foolproof. Sure mistakes are made but the process for fixing them works.

This monograph is an attempt to demonstrate by example how one mathematician reasons. This is done by investigating two topics. One, “Recursive Functions”, builds a generalized equation-solving function. The other, “Comparing Infinities”, compares the sizes of infinite sets. Each topic starts out at an elementary level and builds on itself. All that is needed to read this material is a bit of talent , or grit, and a high school education that included algebra and graphing functions.

That said, “Stepping into Mathematics” will be most useful as a supplement for those college students who find their mathematics education doesn't provide enough insight into mathematical reasoning.

The Manitou Incline is a stairway of some 2700 steps that take one over half way up Pikes Peak in Colorado. It is a popular hike, but not for everybody. I see this monograph as analogous. However there are actually two stairways herein. The second more rigorous than the first.

When climbing these stairways you will encounter, now and then, a question that ends with ??. This is to encourage you to stop and think before going on to read the answer. If you never stop to think things through for yourself, you will not build the understanding needed to continue reading. Give yourself time to acclimate as you climb upwards.
