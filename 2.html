What I Do

I spend part of my time teaching creative problem solving in mathematics and computer science to online students. Using collaborative tools from Google, I offer individualized instruction.

Mathematics and computer programming are good subjects for teaching problem solving because they force a student to be creative within precise constraints. Creativity involves putting ideas together in new ways. Small children can do that. The trick is to make the pieces fit together harmoniously. This is a difficult trick to learn. Everybody deserves a class that helps with the learning. (Why?)

I have decades of experience helping students learn to create correct answers to problems in college-level mathematics and computer science. I do this by focusing on individual needs and de-emphasizing any particular curriculum. Before doing this online, I did it for high school students as well as for practicing programmers in a M.Sc. program.

My online courses last fourteen weeks and consist of two 55 minute contacts per week. Students are expected to make a substantial effort on their own but I am available by email when an extra hint is needed.
